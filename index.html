Marks

  • Points indicating different batted balls in the 2015 MLB
  • Lines indicating the general distribution of different hits (ground balls, line drives, fly balls)

Channels

  • Position, both horizontally and vertically, as points are plotted based on both launch angle and speed off of the bat
  • Position is a good choice since it clearly shows distribution based on the two quantitatie attributes on the axes
  • Color indicating score value
  • Color is an excellent choice, as it draws attention to the most desirable hit (home runs) area of the graph where you can then determine the optimal launch angle and exit velocity

Colormap(s)

  • Divergent colormap where two colors (purple and oragne) indicate the extremes of a range of hits (abover or below average) and white is a neutral color in the middle that indicates average
  • A divergent colormap is ideal for a scenario with a "middle" zone, so in this case of plotting quantitative attributes, it is valuable to see how the most successful hits scored
